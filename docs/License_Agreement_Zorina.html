This Technology Pilot License Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of the Effective Date by and between the Developer and the Pilot Partner. The purpose of this Agreement is to govern the pilot deployment, testing, and evaluation of the Developer's proprietary AI-powered business intelligence platform ("Platform") in a live business environment.

1. Background & Purpose

1.1 The Developer has created a novel AI-powered SaaS platform for business intelligence, analytics, customer lifecycle management, referral tracking, and automated business recommendations for service-based businesses.

1.2 The Platform incorporates proprietary technology covered by a provisional patent application (8 subsystems, 40 claims), including innovations in snapshot-based commission economics, multi-layer referral fraud prevention, booking-to-payment reconciliation, precomputed customer intelligence (65+ metrics), and AI-driven business analysis.

1.3 The Pilot Partner agrees to serve as the first live deployment partner for the Platform, providing access to real business data in order to validate the Platform's technology, accuracy, and business value in a production environment.

1.4 This is a non-commercial pilot engagement. No fees are charged during the Pilot Term. The Developer receives no monetary compensation. The value exchanged is: the Developer gains real-world validation of the technology; the Pilot Partner gains access to advanced analytics and business intelligence capabilities not available through any existing commercial product.

2. Definitions

"Platform" means the Developer's cloud-based application for business intelligence, analytics, customer management, referral tracking, and AI-powered business recommendations, accessible via web browser and mobile devices.

"Pilot Partner Data" means all data imported from the Pilot Partner's Square account, including but not limited to: customer records, booking history, payment transactions, team member information, and service catalogs.

"Derived Analytics" means insights, metrics, reports, segmentation data, and AI-generated recommendations produced by the Platform from processing Pilot Partner Data.

"Authorized Users" means the Pilot Partner's owners, managers, and administrators who are granted access to the Platform.

3. Grant of License

3.1 The Developer grants the Pilot Partner a non-exclusive, non-transferable, revocable license to access and use the Platform for the Pilot Partner's internal business operations during the Pilot Term.

3.2 This license covers all locations currently operated by the Pilot Partner (2 locations as of the Effective Date).

3.3 The Pilot Partner may not sublicense, resell, distribute, or provide access to the Platform to any third party without the Developer's prior written consent.

4. Pilot Term & Cost

4.1 Pilot Term. This Agreement is effective for a period of one (1) year from the Effective Date ("Pilot Term"), beginning March 23, 2026 and ending March 22, 2027.

4.2 No Fees. The Platform is provided at no cost to the Pilot Partner for the entire duration of the Pilot Term. This is a technology validation and testing engagement, not a commercial transaction.

4.3 Renewal. Upon expiration of the Pilot Term, the parties may negotiate a commercial license agreement at mutually agreed terms, or extend the pilot by written amendment.

4.4 Early Termination. Either party may terminate this Agreement with 30 days' written notice at any time during the Pilot Term.

5. Pilot Partner Obligations

The Pilot Partner agrees to:

  1. Provide access to business data via Square API authorization (OAuth 2.0) for Platform integration
  2. Designate at least one Authorized User to evaluate Platform features and provide feedback
  3. Provide reasonable feedback on Platform accuracy, usability, and business value upon Developer's request
  4. Report any data discrepancies, errors, or bugs encountered during use
  5. Allow the Developer to reference this pilot deployment as a case study (without disclosing confidential financial data) for purposes including but not limited to patent applications, investor presentations, and academic research

6. Data Rights & Ownership

6.1 Pilot Partner Data Ownership. The Pilot Partner retains all rights, title, and interest in Pilot Partner Data. The Developer does not claim ownership of any Pilot Partner Data.

6.2 Platform & IP Ownership. The Developer retains all rights, title, and interest in the Platform, including all software, algorithms, analytics engines, AI models, user interfaces, documentation, and any intellectual property therein. The Platform is protected by a provisional patent application (40 claims, 8 subsystems).

6.3 License to Process. The Pilot Partner grants the Developer a limited license to access, process, store, and analyze Pilot Partner Data solely for the purpose of providing the Platform's services and improving the Platform's technology.

6.4 Derived Analytics. Analytics, insights, and AI-generated recommendations produced by the Platform are jointly benefited: the Pilot Partner may use them for business decisions; the Developer may use anonymized, aggregated data to improve the Platform's algorithms and for research purposes.

6.5 Data Portability. Upon termination, the Pilot Partner may request an export of their data in standard format (CSV/JSON) within 30 days.

7. Data Security & Privacy

7.1 The Developer shall implement and maintain commercially reasonable security measures to protect Pilot Partner Data, including: encrypted data transmission (TLS), encrypted data at rest, access controls, and regular security audits.

7.2 The Developer shall not sell, share, or disclose Pilot Partner Data to third parties except as necessary to provide the Platform's services (e.g., Square API integration, SMS delivery via Twilio, email delivery via SendGrid).

7.3 The Developer shall comply with applicable data protection laws, including the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as applicable.

7.4 In the event of a data breach affecting Pilot Partner Data, the Developer shall notify the Pilot Partner within 72 hours of discovery.

8. Square Integration

8.1 The Platform connects to the Pilot Partner's Square account via Square's official OAuth 2.0 and API protocols.

8.2 The Pilot Partner is responsible for maintaining an active Square account. The Developer is not responsible for changes to Square's API or service disruptions on Square's end.

8.3 The Pilot Partner may revoke Square API access at any time.

9. Acceptable Use

The Pilot Partner agrees not to:

  1. Reverse engineer, decompile, or attempt to extract the source code of the Platform
  2. Use the Platform to process data for businesses other than the Pilot Partner's own
  3. Attempt to circumvent security measures, access controls, or rate limits
  4. Use the Platform's referral system for fraudulent purposes (self-referrals, fake accounts)
  5. Share login credentials with unauthorized individuals

10. Intellectual Property Protection

10.1 The Pilot Partner acknowledges that the Platform contains proprietary technology protected by provisional patent application, trade secrets, and copyright.

10.2 The Pilot Partner shall not disclose the Platform's technical architecture, algorithms, analytics methodologies, or AI recommendation logic to competitors or third parties.

10.3 The Pilot Partner may reference the Platform and share their own analytics results publicly but may not represent the Platform's capabilities as their own technology.

11. Limitation of Liability

11.1 THE PLATFORM IS PROVIDED "AS IS" FOR PILOT AND TESTING PURPOSES. THE DEVELOPER MAKES NO WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY OR FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE.

11.2 AI-generated recommendations are advisory in nature. The Pilot Partner is solely responsible for business decisions made based on Platform insights.

11.3 As this is a no-cost pilot engagement, the Developer's total liability under this Agreement shall not exceed $500.

11.4 In no event shall the Developer be liable for indirect, incidental, consequential, or punitive damages, including lost profits or lost data.

12. Confidentiality

12.1 Both parties agree to maintain the confidentiality of information designated as confidential by the disclosing party.

12.2 Confidential information includes but is not limited to: business metrics, customer data, Platform architecture, analytics algorithms, and the terms of this Agreement.

12.3 Confidentiality obligations survive termination of this Agreement for 2 years.

13. General Provisions

13.1 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of California.

13.2 Dispute Resolution. Any disputes shall first be resolved through good-faith negotiation. If unresolved within 30 days, disputes shall be submitted to binding arbitration in San Francisco, California.

13.3 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior discussions and agreements.

13.4 Amendments. This Agreement may only be amended in writing signed by both parties.

13.5 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

13.6 Assignment. Neither party may assign this Agreement without the other party's written consent, except in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all assets.
